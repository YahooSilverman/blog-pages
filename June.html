A Quick One

Good evening, It’s me…Yahoo Silverman. I’m writing tonight to try and make sense of the frustrating electronic signals that zoom around my prefrontal cortex like a one-thousand-man ping pong match. Moment to moment I can be okay. However, if my days were songs then the uplifting verses are always followed by falls of depression and loneliness exclusively in minor keys. I feel as though I’ve wasted and continue to waste a lot of potential. I can write this piece and fill it with exciting ad-verbs and interesting metaphors, I coded this website, I can feel brave enough to post this. But, I always feel as though I’m holding or am held back, not by some extrinsic force…but by myself. I feel like a fish that learned to grow wings but not to breathe air.

Some moments I feel loved and cared for, but when the room quiets and I become cognizant of the fact that I am alone I will feel burdensome. I’ll feel as though my friends are promises never kept, and that I’m a secret not worth sharing. While I’m laying in my bed wishing the sun would set a little faster, I’ll begin feeling the trepidation growing from a small gnaw to a gum deep bite. I want to do better and be happier without relying on constant recognition and reassurance. I want to stop letting conversations that never happened fill the pages of my book.
